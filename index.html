Special Cell Parts

Cell Walls

Protects the cell from injury and gives the cell a regular shape. Absent in animal cells

Chloroplasts

Traps light and energy and carry out photosynthesis. Makes food for cell/organism, present in leaf cell and certain unicellular cells

Vacuole

Stores water, mineral salts and nutrients within the cell. Plant cells have a large central vacuole. Animal cells have small and many vacuoles

Specialised Cells

There are many specialised cells in organisms

Process for this development is called differentiation

Differentiation is the process by which a cell becomes specialised for a specific function

Cells - Specialised for a particular function

Tissue - A group of similar cells which work together to perform a specific function

Organ- Different tissues working together to carry out a specific function

Organ System - Several related organs working together to carry out a particular function

Allows different parts of a plant or an animal to perform various functions in a coordinated way

Allows the organism to function efficiently and effectively at each level

Understand the Basics First

Before diving into complex topics, make sure you have a solid understanding of the fundamental concepts. Science often builds upon earlier knowledge, so a strong foundation is essential.

Active Learning

Engage actively with the material. Instead of just reading, try to explain concepts in your own words, solve problems, or teach them to someone else. This reinforces your understanding.

Use Visual Aids

Science often involves visual elements like diagrams, charts, and graphs. Utilize these tools to help you grasp complex ideas. Create your own visual aids if needed.

Practice Regularly

Science involves problem-solving, so practice is crucial. Work through example problems and exercises to apply what you've learned. If available, use past exams or quizzes for practice.
